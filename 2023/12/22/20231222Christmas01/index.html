From Steam Sales to Singing Bosses: Christmas Chronicles in Taiwan

Christmas in Taiwan

My engineer friend shared some photos of her Christmas tree collection with me. She spotted tons of beautifully adorned trees in North America and Canada. In fact, a parallel situation happens in Taiwan, too. Whether we are planning for a special dinner at a local restaurant or a holiday, we can easily notice that everywhere has been decorated to exhibit the ambience of Christmas. Obviously, Taiwanese never fail to seize every opportunity to celebrate a holiday.

台灣的聖誕節

我的工程師朋友與我分享了她的聖誕樹收藏的一些照片。她在北美和加拿大看到了大量裝飾精美的聖誕樹。實際上，在台灣也發生了類似的情況。無論我們是在當地餐廳安排特別晚餐還是度假，我們都很容易注意到每個地方都裝飾得滿滿的聖誕氛圍。顯然，台灣人從不錯過慶祝節日的每一個機會。

Introduction

It has been widely criticised that Christmas is becoming too commercial in Western countries. For instance, last night I heard such a line when watching The Golden Girls. The TV series was released in 1985! Likewise, Asian people now face a similar phenomenon. Department stores provide discounts to attract customers, and shopping websites host a virtual outlet to captivate capture their users. For example, the renowned video game platform, Steam, is presenting its winter sale now. It seems the essence of this holiday is to buy more things. No wonder people criticise that Christmas is becoming too commercial.

介紹

長久以來，有人廣泛批評聖誕節在西方國家變得太商業化。例如，昨晚我在看《黃金女郎》時聽到了這樣一句話。這部電視劇於 1985 年首播！同樣，亞洲人現在也面臨著類似的現象。百貨公司提供折扣以吸引顧客，購物網站舉辦虛擬特賣會來吸引用戶。例如，著名的視頻遊戲平台 Steam 目前正在舉辦其冬季促銷活動。似乎這個節日的本質就是多買東西。難怪人們批評聖誕節變得太商業化。

Personal Experiences

I used to spend a lot of money on buying new games during a Steam winter sale. To be honest, most games I purchased are more like sealed in my library: I do not have much time to play them. Last time I logged into Steam was to practise my typing skills through a free-to-play game. I think I won’t buy any new video games during this sale!

Besides shopping and video games, there is an interesting personal experience I would like to share. During my last stint, the boss of the company next to ours was a Christian. Once she threw a party and invited several singers whom she acquainted from the church she usually visits. It was on ‘Christmas Noon,’ because she knew none of her employees were going to stay at the company after a hard-working day, even for a party. It turned out everyone had an extremely noisy afternoon break. What a bizarre Christmas!

個人經驗

我過去常在 Steam 冬季促銷活動期間花很多錢買新遊戲。老實說，我購買的大多數遊戲更像是封存在我的遊戲庫中：我沒有太多時間來玩它們。我上一次登錄 Steam 是為了通過一個免費遊戲練習我的打字技能。我想我不會在這次促銷活動中買任何新的視頻遊戲了！

除了購物和視頻遊戲，我還有一個有趣的個人經歷想分享。在我上一份工作期間，我們公司隔壁公司的老板是一名基督徒。有一次她舉辦了一個派對，邀請了一些她在常去的教堂認識的歌手。那是一個 “聖誕正午”，因為她知道她的員工在辛勤工作的一天之後不會留在公司，即使是參加派對也不會留下。結果大家度過了一個非常嘈雜的下午休息時間。多麼奇怪的聖誕節！

Conclusion

Although Christmas is not an official holiday in Taiwan, most people celebrate the holiday through shopping, having special meals, going on a holiday and doing diverse activities. Whether it is commercial or not in Taiwan, it is now a day for Taiwanese people to share their happiness and have fun!

結論

雖然聖誕節在台灣不是官方節日，但大多數人通過購物、享用特別的餐點、度假和各種活動來慶祝這個節日。無論它是否商業化，對台灣人來說，這是一天分享快樂並開心的日子！
