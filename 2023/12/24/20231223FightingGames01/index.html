Fighting Games, Fossilisation and Frustrating Habits

格鬥遊戲、化石化和令人沮喪的習慣

Introduction

Sometimes I think that playing video games can also be inspiring. Today my friend gave me a lesson when we were playing a fighting game together: In terms of fossilisation, it is not only a jargon for language learning, but also a phenomenon we can observe at all corners.

介紹

有時我認為玩視頻遊戲也可以是一種啟發。今天，當我們一起玩格鬥遊戲時，我的朋友給了我一個教訓：就化石化而言，這不僅僅是語言學習的行話，還是我們可以在各個角落觀察到的現象。

A Lesson of a Fighting Game

Today when I was playing a fighting game with my friend, he found out that I always drop the combos due to a small habit: unnecessary poke. A combo in fighting games means a set of consecutive attacks that deals loads of damage to the opponent. A poke, in fighting games, literally means what you do to attack your enemy in a safer way, or to pose a potential threat.

格鬥遊戲的一課

今天，當我和朋友一起玩格鬥遊戲時，他發現我總是因為一個小習慣而失誤：不必要的 poke。在格鬥遊戲中，combo 指的是一套連續的攻擊，對對手造成大量傷害。在格鬥遊戲中，poke 的意思是以更安全的方式進行攻擊，或者構成潛在威脅。

If we compare the situation of playing fighting games with learning a language, it may not be so straightforward. It is true that fossilisation of pronunciation is difficult to overcome, it takes endeavour from experienced teachers and determined students to tackle. However, besides pronunciation of a second language, there is a phenomenon that has been neglected for a long time: overuse of fillers.

It is acceptable to use fillers like ‘well’ or ‘you know’ occasionally in conversation. Filler words are used to express speakers’ uncertainty or to give them more time to think. It functions extremely similar to ‘pokes’ in fighting games: to earn more time. However, seldom do people find that they are overusing it. For instance, shooting a fireball to poke my opponent is a good way to gain information: Will he dodge it? Will he jump over it? Shooting three fireballs consecutively, which is my case, is telling the opponent: this guy is too naive. Jump in and knock him out!

如果我們將玩格鬥遊戲的情況與學習語言相比較，可能就沒那麼簡單了。沉澱的發音確實難以克服，需要經驗豐富的教師和堅定的學生一同努力來解決。然而，除了第二語言的發音，還有一個長期被忽視的現象：過度使用填充詞。

在對話中偶爾使用像 “嗯” 或 “你知道” 的填充詞是可以接受的。填充詞用於表達講者的不確定性，或者給他們更多的思考時間。它的功能與格鬥遊戲中的 “poke” 非常相似：為了贏得更多的時間。然而，很少有人意識到他們正在過度使用它。例如，連續發射三個火球，這就是我的情況，等於告訴對手：這傢伙太天真了。跳過來，擊倒他！

The same situation happens when I practise English speaking with an AI. I start nearly every utterance with ‘well’ or ‘you know.’ The robot does not complain, but how annoying it is to have a small talk with a person keep saying ‘well, you know!’

In short, this is my reflection on today’s gameplay. It is kind of frustrating. Maybe I should put more attention when I speak, or, well, consult my AI!

當我和人工智能練習英語口語時，情況也是一樣的。我幾乎每次發言都以 “嗯” 或 “你知道” 開頭。機器人沒有抱怨，但和一個一直說 “嗯，你知道” 的人閑聊是多麼討厭啊！

總之，這是我對今天遊戲的反思。有點令人沮喪。也許我說話時應該更加注意，或者，嗯，諮詢一下我的人工智能！
