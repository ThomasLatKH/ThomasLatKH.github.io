Beyond Words: The Melody of Dialects in Chinese Linguistic Heritage

Dialect Complexity

方言的複雜性

Introduction

There is a renowned statement in Chinese-speaking community that Taiwanese Southern Min and Cantonese dialects serve as the most beautiful language to recite poems. The reason is that these two dialects preserve significant amounts of Old Chinese or Middle Chinese features. In fact, my high school Chinese teacher once recited a classical poem in Taiwanese Southern Min in the class. The poetry was written by a Tang dynasty Chinese poet, which means that it is at least 1200 years old. Listening to a professional Chinese teacher reading aloud a classical poem in a dialect indeed provided an unusual feeling. That’s why I can still recall the theme vividly. However, it is insufficient to claim that Southern Min or Cantonese is the best language for reciting classical Chinese literature.

簡介

在華語社區中有一句著名的說法，即台灣閩南語和廣東話方言是吟詩最美的語言。原因在於這兩種方言保留了大量古漢語或中古漢語的特徵。事實上，我的高中中文老師曾在課堂上用台灣閩南語吟誦一首古典詩。這首詩是唐代中國詩人所作，意味著至少有1200年的歷史。聆聽一位專業的中文老師用方言朗讀古典詩確實帶來一種不同尋常的感覺。這就是為什麼我仍然能生動地回憶起這首詩的主題。然而，僅憑此不能斷言閩南語或廣東話是吟誦古典中國文學的最佳語言。

Mother Tongue = Taiwanese Southern Min?

First and foremost, I want to share my thought on the mother tongue issues. Most Taiwanese would emphasise the importance of our mother tongue, Taiwanese Southern Min. Moreover, because of political reasons, speaking Taiwanese Southern Min was forbidden in public during the martial law period, which ended about 40 years ago. Nevertheless young people believe that our mother tongue is dying.

Taiwan is a small island which has experienced centuries of colonisation from Dutch, Portuguese, Chinese to Japanese. That fact is that most Taiwanese, including me, are descendants of ancient Han Chinese pioneers or coloniser. There is no politically correct reason to claim that only Taiwanese Southern Min is in danger. The truth is there are tons of indigenous languages craving for preservation in Taiwan. Most of them belong to Austronesian languages, a language family widely spoken throughout islands of the Pacific Ocean.

母語 = 台灣閩南語？

首先，我想分享一下關於母語問題的想法。大多數台灣人會強調我們的母語，即台灣閩南語的重要性。此外，由於政治原因，在大約40年前結束的戒嚴時期，禁止在公共場合使用台灣閩南語。然而，年輕人認為我們的母語正在消失。

台灣是一個小島，歷經荷蘭、葡萄牙、中國和日本的幾個世紀的殖民。事實上，包括我在內的大多數台灣人都是古漢族先鋒或殖民者的後裔。聲稱只有台灣閩南語處於危險中是沒有政治正確性的。事實上，台灣有大量的原住民語言需要保存。它們大多屬於南島語系，這是一個在太平洋島嶼上廣泛使用的語言家族。

Cantonese

A parallel example is Cantonese people also like to emphasise the beauty of their dialect. Likewise, teachers or influencers sometimes recite classical poetry in Cantonese, exhibiting the phonological elegance of their mother tongue. It is easy to understand, for people always think what they have is the best. For instance, Taiwanese people are incredibly proud to be the only community using Traditional Chinese, the authentic legacy of Chinese culture.

廣東話

平行的例子是廣東人也喜歡強調他們方言的美。同樣，老師或影響者有時會用廣東話吟誦古詩，展示他們母語的語音優雅。這是容易理解的，因為人們總是認為他們所擁有的是最好的。例如，台灣人以成為唯一使用繁體字的社區而感到無比自豪，這是中國文化的真正遺產。

The Heritage of Ancient Chinese

I am not an expert, but through some articles and Wikipedia I learnt that both Taiwanese Southern Min and Cantonese succeed some features of ancient Chinese. I think that is proportionately why both Taiwanese Southern Min and Cantonese sound different from the Mandarin Chinese. The dialect study is indeed a treasure trove for linguists!

古漢語的遺產

我不是專家，但通過一些文章和維基百科，我了解到台灣閩南語和廣東話都繼承了一些古漢語的特徵。我認為這可能是為什麼台灣閩南語和廣東話聽起來與普通話有所不同的比例。方言研究確實是語言學家的寶庫！
